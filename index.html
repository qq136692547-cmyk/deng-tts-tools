The hidden TikTok Shop fee stack

01 / what's missing
[ 8% ]

Base commission

This is the one TikTok shows you. 5%–?0% by category, applied to the total sale price including shipping. The headline number, but only part of the story.

[ 2% ]

AFFF platform fee

Application Fund Fee. TikTok's separate 2% on every transaction. Most sellers miss this because it shows up as a "platform fee" line item, not a commission.

[ 1.8% ]

Payment processing

Paid to the payment processor (Stripe/Adyen). Min $0.30. Plus creator commission (1–?0%) if you use TikTok affiliates —?that stacks on top of everything.

FAQ

03 / questions
Why is my TikTok Shop profit different from the TikTok Seller Center dashboard?

The TikTok dashboard shows the gross amount after their commission, but doesn't subtract the AFFF platform fee, payment processing fee, or creator commission separately. Once you add those, your real take-home is typically 12–?5% lower than the dashboard shows.

Is 8% the only commission TikTok takes?

No. The 8% is the base commission. On top of that, TikTok charges a 2% AFFF platform fee and a 1.8% payment processing fee. For a $30 product, the real TikTok fee stack is closer to $3.50 (11.6%) —?not $2.40 (8%).

Do I pay creator commission on top of TikTok's fees?

Yes. If you set a 10% creator commission, that 10% goes to the TikTok influencer on top of the 8% base + 2% AFFF + 1.8% payment. For a $30 product, that's $6.50 in fees (21.7%) —?and you still have to cover COGS, shipping, and returns.

How does TikTok Shop compare to Amazon FBA?

On a per-fee basis, TikTok's headline 8% is much lower than Amazon's 15% referral. But once you include AFFF + payment + creator commission, the gap closes. Use our side-by-side calculator to model the same product on both platforms.
