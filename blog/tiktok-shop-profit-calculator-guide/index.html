How to Calculate TikTok Shop Profit (2026)

June 29, 2026 · 6 min read

If you're sourcing products for TikTok Shop, the first number you need to know is not the selling price — it's your real net profit per unit. Most sellers estimate based on the 6% referral fee and wonder later why their margin disappeared.

This guide walks through the actual calculation with a real example.

The Full TikTok Shop Fee Stack (2026)

TikTok Shop charges the following fees per transaction (US market, 2026):

FeeRateNotes
Referral fee6%Flat for most US categories; already includes payment processing.
FBT fulfillment$2.86–$3.58/unitMandatory as of Mar 31, 2026.
Creator commission0–30%Only if running affiliates.

Before creator commission, the platform take is 6% of sale price plus FBT — not just the headline 6%.

Step-by-Step Calculation

Let's use a real example: a beauty product selling for $29.99 with $9.50 COGS (including shipping).

Step 1: Calculate Total Fees

Referral fee (6%):       $29.99 × 0.06 = $1.80
FBT fulfillment:          ~$3.20 (single-item)
Creator commission (10%): $29.99 × 0.10 = $3.00
Total fees:              $8.00

Step 2: Subtract COGS

Selling price:       $29.99
Total fees:         −$8.00
COGS + shipping:    −$9.50
NET PROFIT:         $12.49 per unit

If you'd only used the 6% commission number, you'd have estimated $20+ per unit. The real net is $12.49 — because FBT and creator commission add up fast.

What Changes at Different Price Points

The fee stack percentage stays roughly the same, but the absolute dollar impact grows with price. A $50 product with 15% creator commission would have about $13.70 in fees before COGS (6% referral + 15% creator + ~$3.20 FBT).

This is why accurate calculation matters more at higher price points — the gap between estimated and real profit gets larger.
