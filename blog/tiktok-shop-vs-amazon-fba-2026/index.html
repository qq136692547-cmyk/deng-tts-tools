TikTok Shop vs Amazon FBA 2026: Which Platform Actually Pays You More?

If you're an e-commerce seller choosing between TikTok Shop and Amazon FBA in 2026, the fee comparison isn't as simple as "TikTok charges 6% and Amazon charges 15%." Each platform has a multi-layer fee stack, creator/affiliate costs, fulfillment mandates, and hidden surcharges that dramatically change your actual take-home per unit.

We'll walk through both platforms' 2026 fee schedules with real product examples. You'll see exactly where every dollar of margin goes before you source your next SKU.

Platform Overview

Here's how the two platforms actually differ before we get into the fees. Amazon FBA is a mature marketplace with 310+ million active customer accounts globally and built-in search traffic. TikTok Shop is a social commerce platform that relies on viral content and creator partnerships rather than search-based discovery.

"TikTok Shop is significantly cheaper than Amazon FBA in raw fees," EcomCalcTools reported in May 2026, noting that "TikTok's total fees are typically 5–10% (commission + processing), while Amazon FBA can consume 25–40% of revenue when you include referral fees, fulfillment, storage, and surcharges." However, TikTok's creator commission model can quickly erase that advantage.

TikTok Shop Fee Stack (2026)

TikTok Shop's fee structure in the US market for 2026 stacks several costs on top of the 6% referral fee. Understanding each one is essential before you calculate your real margin.

1. Referral Fee (6%)

For most US categories — Fashion, Beauty, Home, Food — TikTok charges a flat 6% referral fee on the total order value, including any platform-funded discounts (but excluding tax). Some categories differ: Precious Jewelry is 5%, and select Electronics in EU markets are 7%. New sellers get a 3% promotional rate for the first 30 days (source: Dashboardly, Feb 2026).

2. FBT Fulfillment ($2.86–$3.58/unit)

As of March 31, 2026, TikTok made Fulfilled by TikTok (FBT) mandatory for all US sellers, ending the independent shipping option. FBT costs range from $2.86 to $3.58 per unit, depending on size tier and weight (source: Dashboardly, Apr 2026).

3. Creator Commission (0–30%)

This is the most variable cost. You set the commission rate (1–50%) that TikTok influencers earn when they sell your product through affiliate links. Typical ranges by category: Beauty 15–30%, Fashion 10–15%, Home 12–18%, Tech 5–10% (source: FastMoss, Jun 2026). For products not using affiliate creators, this cost is zero.

4. Returns & Chargebacks

Return rates on TikTok Shop tend to be higher than Amazon, averaging 15–25% for apparel and 8–12% for home goods, compared to Amazon's 5–15% average (source: Darkroom Agency, Jun 2026). Sellers cover 20% of return shipping if their Shop Performance Score is ≥ 4, and 50% if below.

Amazon FBA Fee Stack (2026)

Amazon FBA's fee structure in 2026 includes at least six distinct fees. The complexity means most sellers underestimate their true cost per unit.

1. Referral Fee (8–20%)

Amazon charges a category-dependent referral fee. Key rates: Electronics 8%, Home & Kitchen 15%, Apparel 17%, Jewelry 20%, Tools 15%, Sports 15% (source: FBAlytic, 2026). Most standard categories fall at 15%.

2. FBA Fulfillment Fee ($3.06–$75.78/unit)

Updated January 15, 2026, FBA fulfillment fees range from $3.06 for small standard items to over $75 for large bulky units. A typical large standard item (8–12 oz) costs approximately $4.10 per unit (source: Eightx, 2026). An additional 3.5% fuel and inflation surcharge was applied to all base fulfillment fees starting April 17, 2026.

3. Low-Inventory Level Fee ($0.32–$2.09/unit)

Amazon charges this fee when your historical days of supply at the FNSKU level drops below 28 days. Rates range from $0.32 for small standard to $2.09 for bulky items. Originally limited to standard-size products, this fee was expanded to bulky items in January 2026 (source: Seller Snap, Apr 2026).

4. Inbound Placement Fee ($0.30–$2.30/unit)

If you choose the minimal shipment split option (shipping to a single Amazon warehouse), you pay an inbound placement fee of $0.30 (small standard) to $2.30 (large bulky) per unit. Amazon-optimized splits are fee-free but require shipping to multiple locations.

5. Monthly Storage Fee ($0.78–$2.40/cu ft)

Standard-size storage costs $0.78/cu ft (Jan–Sep) and $2.40/cu ft (Oct–Dec). Oversize items cost $0.56–$1.30/cu ft depending on season. An aged inventory surcharge applies to units stored 181+ days.

6. Returns Processing Fee

Amazon charges a returns processing fee for products in certain categories (apparel, shoes, jewelry, luggage). The fee is typically $2–$6 per returned unit, deducted from the seller's account.

When Each Platform Wins

Choose TikTok Shop when:

  • Your product is visually appealing and priced $20–$60 (the impulse-buy sweet spot)
  • You can produce or source engaging video content
  • You plan to run low or zero creator commissions
  • You're targeting Gen Z and Millennial shoppers
  • You want faster onboarding with no monthly subscription

Choose Amazon FBA when:

  • Your product is a search-driven purchase (e.g., "bathroom scale" or "coffee maker")
  • You have stable, predictable demand and can maintain 28+ days of inventory
  • You're selling higher-priced items ($60+) where FBA's trust signals convert better
  • You want consistent, repeatable sales without depending on viral content
  • You sell products with low return rates (under 10%)

Hidden Costs Most Sellers Miss

Beyond the fees shown above, both platforms have costs that aren't immediately obvious:

TikTok Shop Hidden Costs

  • Content production: Expect to spend $500–$2,000/month on video content, or significant time if producing in-house
  • Higher return rates: TikTok's impulse-buy model drives 15–25% return rates for fashion, compared to Amazon's 5–15%
  • Ad spend: To get consistent sales without relying on organic virality, many sellers spend 15–25% of revenue on TikTok Shop Ads
  • Settlement delays: Standard payouts take ~15 days; flagged accounts can wait up to 31 days (source: Dashboardly)

Amazon FBA Hidden Costs

  • Aged inventory surcharge: Units stored 181+ days incur additional fees that escalate at 365 days
  • PPC advertising: Most categories require 10–25% of revenue in ad spend to maintain visibility
  • Prep service fees: If you use Amazon's prep services, add $0.50–$2.00 per unit
  • Long-tail storage: Q4 storage rates (Oct–Dec) are 3x higher than the rest of the year
