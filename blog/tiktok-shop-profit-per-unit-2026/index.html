How to Calculate TikTok Shop Profit Per Unit (Step-by-Step 2026 Guide)

Most TikTok Shop sellers calculate profit by subtracting the 6% referral fee and their product cost, then calling it a day. The problem? That math ignores payment processing, FBT fulfillment, creator commissions, ad spend, and returns — five costs that can cut your actual profit in half.

Here's the full profit-per-unit formula for TikTok Shop in 2026. We'll run it at three price points — $20, $40, and $80 — so you can see exactly where the money goes before you order your first unit.

The Formula

TikTok Shop profit per unit is calculated as: your sale price, minus every fee TikTok collects, minus your product cost, minus creator commissions and ad spend allocated to that unit.

Profit Per Unit = Sale Price − Referral Fee − Payment Processing − FBT Fulfillment − Creator Commission − COGS − Ad Spend Per Unit − Return Reserve

Each component matters. Skip one, and you'll overestimate your margin by 5–15 percentage points — enough to turn a "profitable" product into a money loser at scale. According to Grey Journal's 2026 seller survey, the average active US TikTok Shop seller generates about $3,750 per month in gross sales and takes home approximately $690 in net profit — an 18.4% net margin. Many sellers believe they're doing better because they stop at the referral fee.

Step 1: Start With Gross Revenue

Gross revenue is the total amount the customer pays, before any deductions. If you sell a product for $29.99, your gross revenue per unit is $29.99.

That part's easy. The real work starts when you subtract TikTok's fees.

Step 2: Subtract TikTok's Fee Stack

TikTok Shop charges sellers a multi-layer fee on every transaction. Here are the three mandatory fees:

Referral Fee (6%)

For most US categories — Fashion, Beauty, Home, Food — TikTok charges a flat 6% referral fee on the total order value. This is calculated on the customer payment amount including any platform-funded discounts, but excluding tax (source: Dashboardly, Apr 2026). New sellers get a promotional 3% rate for the first 30 days.

Formula: Referral fee = Sale price × 6%

Payment Processing (~1.8%)

Each transaction includes a processing fee of approximately 1.8%. This varies slightly by payment method but averages 1.5–2.2% for US sellers.

Formula: Payment fee = Sale price × 1.8%

FBT Fulfillment ($2.86–$3.58/unit)

As of March 31, 2026, TikTok made Fulfilled by TikTok mandatory for US sellers. FBT costs range from $2.86 to $3.58 per unit, depending on size tier and weight (source: Dashboardly). If you ship through Upgraded TikTok Shipping, rates are approximately 20% below standard carrier rates.

Note on sales tax: TikTok Shop collects sales tax from the buyer, but as the seller you're responsible for remitting it. It doesn't come out of your profit directly, but it affects your cash flow and pricing strategy — especially if you sell across multiple states with different rates.

Total mandatory fee layer: For a $29.99 product, that's $1.80 (referral) + $0.54 (payment) + $3.20 (FBT) = $5.54 — or roughly 18.5% of your sale price before you've even looked at product cost.

Step 3: Subtract COGS

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) is what you paid to acquire or manufacture the product. This includes the unit price from your supplier, any import duties, packaging, and labeling costs.

For a product you source from a supplier at $5.00 per unit, with $1.50 in duties and $0.50 in custom packaging, your COGS is $7.00 per unit.

Formula: Gross margin after fees = Sale price − Total fees − COGS

Step 4: Factor Creator Commissions

Creator commissions are the percentage you pay TikTok influencers when they sell your product through affiliate links. This is optional — you can set it to 0% — but in practice, most sellers on TikTok Shop rely on creator partnerships for volume.

Typical creator commission rates by category (source: FastMoss, Jun 2026):

CategoryTypical Creator Commission
Beauty15–30%
Fashion10–15%
Home & Kitchen12–18%
Electronics / Tech5–10%
General / Other10–20%

What most sellers miss: Creator commissions are calculated on the sale price before TikTok's fees. So a 10% commission on a $29.99 sale costs you $3.00 — on top of the $5.54 in TikTok fees. That 10% commission effectively comes entirely out of your margin.

Step 5: Subtract Ad Spend

If you run TikTok Shop Ads (formerly GMV Max or PLA campaigns), factor in your ad cost per unit sold. Most sellers spend 15–25% of revenue on TikTok ads (source: Darkroom Agency, Jun 2026).

To calculate ad spend per unit: divide your total monthly ad budget by the number of units sold through ads. If you spend $3,000/month on ads and sell 300 units through ad-attributed orders, your ad cost per unit is $10.00.

Higher-ticket items can get away with a lower ad-to-revenue ratio because the absolute dollar amount is still high enough to win bids. That's why the electronics example below uses 15% while the beauty and home examples use 20%.

Step 6: Account for Returns

Return rates on TikTok Shop are higher than traditional e-commerce because of the impulse-buy nature of the platform. Average return rates by category (source: Darkroom Agency):

  • Apparel: 15–25%
  • Home goods: 8–12%
  • Beauty: 5–10%
  • Electronics: 3–8%

To model returns, multiply your expected return rate multiplied by your total cost per unit including product, fees, fulfillment, creator commissions, and ad spend — everything you lose when a unit comes back. If your all-in cost is $18/unit and you expect 10% returns, add $1.80/unit as your return reserve.

What's a Good Profit Margin on TikTok Shop?

Based on the examples above and industry data, here are target profit margins for TikTok Shop in 2026:

  • Excellent: 25%+ net margin (achievable with $50+ products, low creator commissions)
  • Good: 15–25% net margin (solid for most categories)
  • Average: 10–15% (typical for competitive categories with moderate creator spend)
  • Below average: Under 10% (high creator commissions, low price points, or high ad costs)

The 2026 seller survey data shows the average seller nets 18.4% margin (source: Grey Journal, May 2026). If you're below 10%, you're likely overpaying on creator commissions or sourcing at the wrong price point.

Quick Summary

The math is straightforward once you know all the pieces. A few takeaways from the numbers above:

  • The $20–$50 price range hits the sweet spot on TikTok Shop — high enough that fees don’t eat everything, low enough for impulse buys.
  • Creator commissions are the biggest variable. A 15% commission can turn a 20% margin into 4%. Model this before you launch.
  • Don’t skip ad spend and returns in your calculations. Together they can take 20–30% off your gross revenue.
