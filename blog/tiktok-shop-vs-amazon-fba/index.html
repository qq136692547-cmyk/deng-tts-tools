TikTok Shop vs Amazon FBA: Which Pays You More?

Most "TikTok Shop vs Amazon FBA" articles compare the headline commission rate: 8% vs 15%, TikTok wins. But that comparison is misleading. The real per-unit profit depends on five variables most articles skip. This post runs the same $29.99 product through both platforms with realistic 2026 fees.

The setup

Same SKU, sold for $29.99 in "Home & Kitchen" (or TikTok's "Home" category). $6.50 COGS, $1.20 inbound shipping to Amazon FC, $3.00 fulfillment cost on TikTok Shop. 10% Amazon ad spend, 15% TikTok ad spend (TikTok requires more). 0% creator commission on TikTok.

Amazon FBA breakdown

  • Referral (15%): $4.50
  • FBA fulfillment (large standard, +3.5% fuel surcharge): $5.15
  • Inbound placement fee (partial, standard size): $0.21
  • Low-inventory fee (assuming 28 days supply): $0.35
  • Storage (1 month, non-Q4): ~$0.10
  • Ad spend (10% of sale): $3.00
  • COGS: $6.50
  • Inbound shipping: $1.20
  • Total cost: $21.01
  • Net profit per unit: $8.98 (29.9% margin)

TikTok Shop breakdown

  • Base commission (8%): $2.40
  • AFFF platform fee (2%): $0.60
  • Payment processing (1.8%): $0.54
  • Creator commission (0%): $0.00
  • Ad spend (15%): $4.50
  • COGS: $6.50
  • Shipping: $3.00
  • Total cost: $17.54
  • Net profit per unit: $12.45 (41.5% margin)

The verdict (for this product)

TikTok wins by $3.47 per unit. The big reasons: Amazon's 15% referral + $5 fulfillment + placement + low-inventory fees stack to $10.21 in fees, vs TikTok's $3.54 in fees. The $6.67 fee difference more than makes up for TikTok's higher ad spend and shipping costs.

When the answer flips

Add a 10% creator commission to TikTok and the gap closes. Add a 20% creator commission and Amazon wins. Add 25% creator commission and Amazon wins by $5+ per unit. The creator rate is the single biggest swing variable.

Other factors that change the answer

  • Returns: TikTok runs 15%–25% returns, Amazon 5%–10%. Each TikTok return costs you shipping both ways + a damaged product.
  • Ad cost stability: Amazon ad CPC has been stable for years. TikTok ad CPC has been volatile — single campaigns can blow up your ACOS overnight.
  • Organic reach: Amazon gives you organic search traffic from day 1 (if your listing is optimized). TikTok requires constant video creation to maintain visibility.
  • Inventory risk: Amazon charges long-term storage surcharges on slow movers. TikTok charges none — but you risk getting delisted if a product doesn't move.
  • Time investment: Amazon is more "set and forget." TikTok is more "constant content creation" — expect to spend 2–3x more time per dollar of revenue.

The honest answer

For high-COGS, search-driven products: Amazon wins. For low-COGS, trend-driven, video-friendly products: TikTok wins. Most successful multi-channel sellers run both — Amazon for the long-tail organic demand, TikTok for the trend-driven impulse buys.
